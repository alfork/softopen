Manufacturing Skills Gap Challenge

The Critical Challenge

America stands at a critical juncture in manufacturing history, with an estimated 2.1 million manufacturing jobs potentially going unfilled by 2030. The traditional educational system has struggled to keep pace with the rapid technological advances transforming modern manufacturing, creating a significant disconnect between what students learn and what employers desperately need.

Educational System Gaps

Traditional STEM programs often rely on theoretical instruction with limited practical application, failing to prepare students for the realities of modern manufacturing environments.

Industry Needs

Modern manufacturing facilities frequently operate with idle equipment due to lack of trained personnel who can effectively utilize sophisticated automated systems.

ICR247: Advanced Manufacturing

ICR247 Company Overview

Founded on Innovation

ICR247, founded in 2015 and headquartered in Wheeling, Illinois, represents the practical application cornerstone of this educational revolution. The company specializes in providing small and medium-sized enterprises with state-of-the-art automation solutions that enhance productivity and efficiency.

Dual Mission

What sets ICR247 apart is its dual mission of driving industrial progress while simultaneously advancing educational opportunities. The company operates apprenticeship programs that provide students with paid learning opportunities combining theoretical knowledge with practical application.

Industry Experience

The company's expertise spans CNC machining, robotics, motion control, and factory automation, with over five years of combined industry experience servicing leading brands like Yaskawa.
